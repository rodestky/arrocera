Get to Know Us

Explore a Part of Our History

Year 1906

The company has its origins in the Bertazzi y Barros partnership, which began operations in 1906 in San Fernando as a food distributor and local fruit cellar located at the corner of Manuel Rodríguez and the railway line. Shortly thereafter, storage warehouses were built at Manuel Rodríguez 1170. Simultaneously, the Italiana Pasta Factory was constructed at Chacabuco 222 by the Astrain, Bertazzi, and Gerard Ltda. partnership.

The 1940s

In the early 1940s, the company was sold to the Fedeghelli & Parro partnership, and the pasta factory was acquired by Don Santiago Gandulfo, whose grandson, Don Adolfo Rojas Gandulfo, would later become the president of Banco de Chile. Unfortunately, the Gandulfo pasta factory suffered a voracious fire that left it in ruins to this day.

The 1960s

In the 1960s, Don Cesar Fedeghelli and Don Juan Parro decided to part ways. The heirs of Don Cesar would retain ownership of the Santa Rita Estate and become shareholders of Frusan, while Don Juan Parro and his children would establish Arrocera y Aceitera San Fernando.

The 1980s

In the 1980s, the company went into liquidation, and later the Ramirez family, owners of the former industrial clothing factory Bisonte S.A, took control. They gave a strong boost by using and remodeling the facilities of 8,000 m2 located at Manuel Rodriguez 165 and warehouses in Santiago. In the following years, they acquired the Coagra grain plant of 38,000m2 from Don Juan Sutil and the general offices used by Santander Bank with 1,018 m2 on Huérfanos Street in Santiago Centro. Today, the company imports and distributes food from Arica to Tierra del Fuego, serving a large number of public institutions.

Year 2009

By this year, we finally established ourselves in Washington D.C. with a commercial office and acquired 1,832 sq ft of land for a branch in the port of Baltimore, MD, USA, to begin sales to federal government institutions in the United States through the website www.sam.gov.
